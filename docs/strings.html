techminer.strings.extract_nearby(pattern, x, n_words=1, ignore_case=True, full_match=False, use_re=False)[source]¶

Extracts the words of string x in the proximity of the terms matching the keywords list.

Parameters

  • x (string) – A string object.

  • n_words (integer) – number of words around term.

Returns

String.

Examples

>>> import pandas as pd
>>> df = pd.DataFrame({
...    'f': ['1 2 3 4 5 6', 'aaa 1 2 3 4 5', '1 aaa 2 3 4 5', '1 2 aaa 3 4 5',
...          '1 2 3 aaa 4 5', '1 2 3 4 aaa 5', '1 2 3 4 5 aaa'],
... })
>>> df
               f
0    1 2 3 4 5 6
1  aaa 1 2 3 4 5
2  1 aaa 2 3 4 5
3  1 2 aaa 3 4 5
4  1 2 3 aaa 4 5
5  1 2 3 4 aaa 5
6  1 2 3 4 5 aaa
>>> df.f.map(lambda x: extract_nearby('aaa', x, n_words=2)) 
0           None
1        aaa 1 2
2      1 aaa 2 3
3    1 2 aaa 3 4
4    2 3 aaa 4 5
5      3 4 aaa 5
6        4 5 aaa
Name: f, dtype: object

techminer.strings.find_string(pattern, x, ignore_case=True, full_match=False, use_re=False)[source]¶

Find pattern in string.

Parameters

  • pattern (string) –

  • x (string) –

  • ignore_case (bool) –

  • full_match (bool) –

  • use_re (bool) –

Returns

string or None

>>> find_string(r'\btwo\b', 'one two three four five', use_re=True)
'two'

>>> find_string(r'\bTWO\b', 'one two three four five', use_re=True)
'two'

>>> find_string(r'\btwo\b', 'one TWO three four five', ignore_case=False, use_re=True) is None
True

>>> find_string(r'\btwo\Wthree\b', 'one two three four five', ignore_case=False, use_re=True)
'two three'
